[CRM Technical Developer]

Education and Qualifications

  • Technical Degree: A bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Software Engineering, or Information Technology (IT) provides the necessary foundation in programming, data structures, and software development methodologies.
  • Platform Training: Foundational coursework or intensive training focused specifically on the architecture and APIs of a major CRM platform (e.g., Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics 365, or HubSpot) is essential.
  • Core Programming Background: Knowledge of underlying programming languages relevant to the CRM ecosystem, such as Java or C# for Dynamics, or Apex and Visualforce/Lightning Web Components for Salesforce.

Skills and Knowledge

  • Platform Customization and Development: Expertise in writing code to build custom features, extensions, and complex business logic within the CRM platform, utilizing the platform's proprietary languages and tools.
  • Integration and API Proficiency: Strong knowledge of web services (REST/SOAP) and the ability to design and implement integrations between the CRM system and other enterprise applications (e.g., ERP, marketing automation tools).
  • Data Modeling and Migration: Understanding of the CRM system's data model, proficiency in SQL or other query languages, and the ability to design data structures and execute complex data migration and cleansing tasks.

Certifications

  • Platform Developer Certification: The most crucial certifications are the Developer-track credentials from the specific vendor, such as the Salesforce Platform Developer I & II or the Microsoft Certified: Power Platform Developer Associate.
  • Advanced Developer and Architecture: Higher-level certifications (e.g., Salesforce Certified Technical Architect) can be sought after gaining significant experience, validating mastery of design principles and platform governance.
  • Complementary Technical Certifications: Certifications in related technologies, such as cloud computing (e.g., AWS Certified Developer) or database management, can enhance the developer's profile.

Experience and Training

  • Hands-on Development Projects: Building a portfolio of projects that demonstrate ability to develop custom features, complex workflows, and integrations within a CRM sandbox or development environment.
  • Team Environment Training: Experience working within an Agile/Scrum development team, utilizing version control (Git), and participating in code reviews to ensure quality and adherence to best practices.
  • Exposure to Full Lifecycle: Training or experience participating in the entire Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) for a CRM implementation, from technical design and coding to unit testing, deployment, and post-go-live support.

Key Responsibilities

  • Custom Code Development: Writing, testing, and deploying custom code (e.g., Apex Triggers, C# plugins, JavaScript, or Power Automate flows) to implement complex business logic that cannot be achieved through standard configuration.
  • System Integration and Data Management: Designing and developing APIs and middleware for integrating the CRM with external systems, and managing complex data transformations and migration routines.
  • Technical Design and Governance: Collaborating with Business Analysts and Architects to translate requirements into scalable technical designs, and ensuring solutions adhere to the platform's security, performance, and governance best practices.
