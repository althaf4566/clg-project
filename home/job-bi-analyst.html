Business Intelligence Analyst

Education and Qualifications

  • Relevant Degree: A bachelor's degree in a quantitative field like Computer Science, Information Technology (IT), Statistics, Data Science, or Business Administration (with a focus on analytics) is typically required.
  • Quantitative and Business Foundation: Education should provide a blend of statistical methods, data warehousing concepts, and core business principles (e.g., finance, marketing) to understand the context of the data.

Skills and Knowledge

  • Data Querying and Manipulation: Expert proficiency in Structured Query Language (SQL) for extracting, manipulating, and transforming data from various database sources is essential.
  • Data Visualization and Reporting: Mastery of industry-leading BI tools like Tableau, Microsoft Power BI, or Looker to design, develop, and maintain dynamic reports, dashboards, and visualizations.
  • Analytical and Business Acumen: Strong ability to analyze complex data sets to identify trends, outliers, and root causes, and excellent business acumen to interpret findings and translate them into practical, data-driven recommendations.

Certifications

  • BI Tool Certifications: Obtaining certifications from major BI platform vendors is highly valuable, such as Microsoft Certified: Power BI Data Analyst Associate or the Tableau Desktop Specialist/Certified Associate.
  • Data Fundamentals and SQL: Certifications that validate core skills in data handling, such as the Oracle Database SQL Certified Associate or specialized training in cloud data platforms (e.g., Azure or AWS data services).
  • Business Analysis: Complementary certifications like the IIBA Entry Certificate in Business Analysis (ECBA) can demonstrate structured methodology in gathering and documenting reporting requirements.

Experience and Training

  • Hands-on Tool Practice: Extensive practical training and experience using a chosen BI platform to connect to data sources, perform data modeling, and build end-to-end dashboards that answer specific business questions.
  • SQL and Database Exposure: Gaining experience through projects or entry-level roles (e.g., Data Analyst, Junior DBA) that require writing complex SQL queries and understanding data warehouse and relational database schemas.
  • Business Stakeholder Interaction: Training or experience focused on gathering reporting requirements from business users, managing expectations, and presenting findings to non-technical audiences.

Key Responsibilities

  • Data Transformation and Modeling: Connecting to source systems, writing SQL queries to extract data, and designing robust data models within the BI tool to ensure accurate, performant, and reliable reporting.
  • Reporting and Dashboard Development: Creating and maintaining interactive dashboards, reports, and visualizations that track key performance indicators (KPIs) and provide business stakeholders with actionable insights.
  • Strategic Analysis and Consultation: Conducting deep-dive data analysis to answer strategic business questions (e.g., "Why are sales declining in X region?"), identifying opportunities for process improvement, and presenting findings to management.
