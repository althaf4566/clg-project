E-Commerce Analyst

Education and Qualifications

  • Relevant Bachelor's Degree: A bachelor's degree in Business Administration, Marketing, Economics, Statistics, or Data Science is highly desirable, as it provides a strong foundation in both business principles and quantitative analysis.
  • Quantitative and Business Focus: Education should include coursework emphasizing statistical analysis, business intelligence (BI), and key e-commerce subjects like digital marketing, inventory management, and customer behavior.

Skills and Knowledge

  • Web Analytics Expertise: Mastery of web analytics platforms such as Google Analytics 4 (GA4) or Adobe Analytics, including the ability to configure tracking, create segments, build custom reports, and interpret e-commerce metrics (e.g., Conversion Rate, AOV, Bounce Rate).
  • Data Manipulation and Visualization: Proficiency in SQL for querying databases and experience with data visualization tools like Tableau, Power BI, or Looker to translate complex data sets into actionable business insights.
  • E-commerce and Digital Marketing Knowledge: A strong understanding of the e-commerce customer funnel, search engine optimization (SEO), pay-per-click (PPC), and A/B testing methodologies to drive optimization recommendations.

Certifications

  • Web Analytics Certifications: The most valuable certifications are directly related to the tools used, such as the Google Analytics Certification and certifications for platforms like Adobe Analytics or specific BI tools.
  • Data and Business Intelligence (BI): Certifications in data manipulation and visualization, such as the Microsoft Certified: Power BI Data Analyst Associate or a Tableau Desktop Specialist certification, are highly beneficial.
  • Digital Marketing Fundamentals: Completing professional certificates in digital marketing or search marketing helps validate an understanding of the levers analysts are expected to influence.

Experience and Training

  • Hands-on Tool Experience: Training and experience must focus on the practical use of analytics tools, including setting up e-commerce tracking, funnel analysis, and attribution modeling on a live or simulated e-commerce site.
  • Entry-Level/Related Roles: Starting in roles like Marketing Analyst, Data Analyst, or SEO Specialist provides foundational experience in data analysis and digital performance before specializing in e-commerce.
  • A/B Testing and Optimization: Experience in designing, executing, and analyzing the results of A/B tests on product pages, checkout flows, and landing pages to directly contribute to revenue optimization.

Key Responsibilities

  • Performance Reporting and Insights: Developing and maintaining regular performance reports and dashboards focused on key e-commerce metrics, and proactively identifying trends, issues, and opportunities to improve online sales.
  • Deep Dive Analysis and Optimization: Conducting deep-dive analyses into areas like cart abandonment, traffic source performance, and product affinity, then translating those insights into actionable recommendations for product, marketing, or UX/UI teams.
  • Data Integrity and Implementation: Ensuring the accuracy of web tracking and data collection (e.g., verifying GA4 implementation) and collaborating with development teams to correctly implement new tracking requirements for new features or campaigns.
